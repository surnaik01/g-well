Team

We're a small team of engineers and builders based in India, close to the farms we want to serve. We've spent the last year obsessed with a single question: why are we still discovering crop problems with our eyes, days after the damage has already started?

Instead of staying in labs and slide decks, we're working directly with growers and agronomists to turn AI imaging into something farmers actually trust and want to use.

Problem

Farmers lose a huge chunk of yield every year because diseases are caught too late. By the time leaves turn yellow, get spots, or wilt, the infection has already been spreading for days.

Today's tools are mostly reactive:

  • Manual scouting that can't keep up with large fields.
  • Apps that only detect visible symptoms from RGB photos.
  • Uniform spraying that wastes chemicals and money.

There is no simple way for farmers to see invisible stress or infection before it shows up to the naked eye. That timing gap is where most of the damage (and cost) lives.

Solution

G-well uses AI-powered hyperspectral imaging to spot disease signatures in plants 7–10 days before visible symptoms.

A compact camera unit, mounted on drones or fixed rails, scans fields and captures hundreds of narrow wavelength bands per pixel. Our models learn the biochemical fingerprints of healthy vs. stressed plants, long before the leaf looks "sick".

Under the hood:

  • Vision Transformers trained on labeled spectral leaf data.
  • Time-series models to forecast how infection will spread.
  • A recommendation engine that suggests targeted interventions instead of blanket spraying.

Farmers get a simple view: a field map, early-risk zones, and clear next steps—so they can act early, reduce chemical use, and protect yield instead of just reacting to loss.
